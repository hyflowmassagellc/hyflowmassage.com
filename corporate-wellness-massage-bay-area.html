Why Bay Area Tech Companies Are Adding On-Site Massage to Their Benefits Package

By Tina Zhang, CMT  ·  March 2025  ·  5 min read

The Bay Area has long been a leader in employee wellness benefits — from catered lunches to on-site gyms. In recent years, on-site massage therapy has emerged as one of the most cost-effective and employee-valued wellness investments a company can make. Here's why, and what it looks like in practice.

The numbers are compelling, but the on-the-ground reality is simpler: employees who feel cared for perform better, stay longer, and show up more. In a competitive hiring market like Silicon Valley, a visible wellness benefit like on-site massage sends a clear signal about company culture.

What On-Site Corporate Massage Actually Looks Like

Corporate wellness massage from HyFlow Massage LLC is designed to be fully turnkey for your HR team. Here's what a typical event looks like:

  • We bring everything — professional massage chair, all supplies, and a licensed CMT therapist. You provide a quiet room or corner of your office.
  • Sessions run 10–20 minutes each — employees stay fully clothed. No oils, no setup time for each person.
  • Focus areas: neck, shoulders, upper back, forearms, and hands — the areas most affected by desk work
  • Scheduling: we work with your HR team to set up a booking queue for the day
  • Typical event: 4–8 hours, serving 15–40 employees depending on session length

Tax Deductibility

Corporate wellness massage programs are generally tax-deductible as an employee benefit expense under IRS Section 162. Consult your accountant for specifics, but this effectively reduces the net cost of any program by your corporate tax rate.
