Post-Surgery Recovery

What to Expect from Lymphatic Drainage
After BBL, Liposuction & Tummy Tuck

By Tina Zhang, CMT  ·  June 2025  ·  8 min read

If you've recently had a BBL, liposuction, tummy tuck, or a mommy makeover, your surgeon has likely mentioned lymphatic drainage massage as part of your recovery protocol. But what does it actually do? When do you start? How many sessions do you need? These are the questions we answer for clients every week at our Mountain View studio.

What Is Manual Lymphatic Drainage (MLD)?

Manual Lymphatic Drainage is a specialized technique using very light, rhythmic pressure to stimulate the lymphatic system — the network responsible for removing excess fluid, waste products, and immune cells from your body's tissues. After surgery, the lymphatic system is disrupted. Trauma to tissue causes fluid to accumulate, resulting in swelling, firmness, and discomfort. MLD helps the lymphatic vessels do their job faster — clearing fluid and reducing swelling more efficiently than the body can manage alone.

Important

Lymphatic drainage massage is not the same as a regular massage. The pressure used is extremely light — far lighter than Swedish or deep tissue. Heavy pressure on post-surgical tissue causes more harm than good. Always work with a therapist trained specifically in MLD technique.

When Can You Start?

Timing depends on your surgery. Always follow your surgeon's individual guidance first.

Day 3–7
BBL, Liposuction, Arm Lift, Thigh Lift: Sessions can typically begin once the wound is closed and your surgeon provides written clearance. Early sessions are most impactful for preventing fibrosis and supporting fat cell survival after BBL.
Day 5–10
Tummy Tuck, Mommy Makeover: Clearance required before starting. Sessions reduce fluid accumulation and soften the scar line.
Week 1–2
Breast Augmentation, Reduction, Facelift, Rhinoplasty: Gentle MLD around affected areas; your therapist will avoid direct pressure on incision sites until cleared.
Week 4–6
C-Section, Hysterectomy, Myomectomy: Internal healing must be confirmed by your OB-GYN before abdominal MLD begins.
Week 2–4
Mastectomy, Axillary Node Dissection: Typically begins after drains are removed. Early MLD is critical for preventing lymphedema.

Written physician clearance is required before your first session at HyFlow Massage LLC.

How Many Sessions Do You Need?

  • Weeks 1–4: 2–3 sessions per week, especially if swelling is significant
  • Weeks 5–8: 1–2 sessions per week as swelling resolves
  • Month 3+: Monthly maintenance sessions to address remaining firmness or fibrosis

What to Expect During a Session

Your first session begins with a review of your physician clearance and a conversation about your surgery, recovery progress, and symptoms. The massage itself uses extremely light touch — often described as a gentle skin stretch. There should be no pain. Sessions run 60–90 minutes for post-surgical clients.

What you may feel after your session

  • Visible reduction in puffiness and swelling
  • Softer, less rigid tissue in treated areas
  • Increased need to urinate — the body is expelling cleared fluid
  • Mild fatigue — normal and indicates the lymphatic system is active

Why Early Treatment Prevents Fibrosis

Fibrosis is the hardening of tissue that develops when post-surgical swelling is left unmanaged. It feels like firm, rope-like bands under the skin — particularly common after liposuction and tummy tuck. Starting MLD within the first week of clearance significantly reduces fibrosis risk and supports smoother, faster healing.
