Can You Use HSA or FSA for Massage Therapy? Yes — Here's How

By Tina Zhang, CMT  ·  April 2025  ·  5 min read

One of the most common questions we receive from new clients is whether they can pay for massage therapy using their HSA (Health Savings Account) or FSA (Flexible Spending Account) funds. The short answer is yes — in most cases. Here's what you need to know.

Is Massage Therapy an Eligible HSA/FSA Expense?

Massage therapy is considered a qualified medical expense under IRS guidelines when it is prescribed by a licensed physician to treat a specific medical condition. This means the massage must be medically necessary — not simply for general relaxation or wellness.

Conditions that commonly qualify include: chronic back pain, neck pain, sciatica, tension headaches, post-surgical recovery, lymphedema, anxiety (with physician documentation), repetitive strain injuries, and musculoskeletal conditions diagnosed by a doctor.

Key Requirement

To use HSA/FSA for massage, you typically need a Letter of Medical Necessity (LMN) from your physician stating that massage therapy is prescribed for a specific condition. Some HSA/FSA administrators may also accept a doctor's referral or prescription. Check with your plan administrator for their specific documentation requirements.

How to Use Your HSA or FSA at HyFlow Massage LLC

1

Get a Letter of Medical Necessity

Ask your primary care physician, specialist, or surgeon to write an LMN stating that massage therapy is prescribed for your condition. This is a simple letter — most doctors are familiar with the request.

2

Pay with Your HSA/FSA Card

We accept HSA and FSA debit cards directly at checkout — just like a regular debit card. No additional steps required at the time of payment.

3

Request a Superbill

If your HSA/FSA administrator or insurance requires documentation, ask us for a superbill after your session. We provide superbills with all required information including CPT codes, session date, provider name and license number, and NPI.

4

Submit for Reimbursement (If Needed)

Submit the superbill and your LMN to your HSA/FSA administrator or insurance plan for reimbursement. Approval timelines and coverage amounts vary by plan.

CPT Codes Used at HyFlow Massage LLC

For insurance and HSA/FSA documentation, massage therapy is billed using standardized CPT (Current Procedural Terminology) codes. The specific codes applied depend on the type of service:

What About Out-of-Network Insurance?

HyFlow Massage LLC also works with clients who have out-of-network insurance benefits. If your plan includes out-of-network coverage for massage therapy or manual therapy, you may be able to submit a superbill for partial reimbursement.

We recommend calling your insurance provider before your first session and asking specifically:

  • Does my plan cover out-of-network massage therapy (CPT 97124) or manual therapy (CPT 97140)?
  • Is a physician referral or LMN required for coverage?
  • What is my out-of-network deductible and reimbursement percentage?
  • How do I submit a superbill for reimbursement?
