Why Post-Surgery Massage Matters

Surgery — whether cosmetic or reconstructive — creates significant trauma to the body's tissues. The body responds with swelling (edema), bruising, inflammation, and fluid accumulation as part of its natural healing process. While this is normal, unmanaged post-surgical fluid can lead to complications including fibrosis (hardening of tissue), prolonged recovery, irregular contour, and scar tissue formation.

Post-surgery lymphatic drainage massage is the most effective non-invasive intervention for managing these complications. By manually stimulating the lymphatic vessels, we accelerate the removal of excess fluid, reduce inflammation, prevent fibrosis, and support the body's natural healing process — leading to faster, smoother, more comfortable recovery.

Surgeries We Support

BBL Recovery Massage (Brazilian Butt Lift)

BBL recovery is one of the most demanding post-surgical recovery processes, requiring careful management of swelling and fluid in the abdomen, flanks, and buttocks. Lymphatic drainage is essential for BBL recovery — it reduces swelling, prevents fluid pockets (seromas), and helps achieve the smooth, defined result your surgeon worked to create.

We recommend beginning BBL lymphatic drainage 5–7 days post-procedure, with surgeon clearance. Most clients benefit from daily or every-other-day sessions for the first two weeks, followed by weekly sessions through week 6.

Liposuction Recovery Massage

Liposuction disrupts significant amounts of lymphatic tissue, making post-lipo lymphatic drainage particularly important. Without proper drainage, swelling can persist for months and fibrosis can develop in the treated areas, creating lumpy, uneven texture. Post-lipo massage dramatically reduces these risks and helps achieve the smooth contour you expected from your procedure.

Tummy Tuck (Abdominoplasty) Recovery

Abdominoplasty involves significant tissue removal and repositioning, creating extensive swelling in the abdominal area. Lymphatic drainage after tummy tuck helps manage this swelling, reduces the sensation of tightness, and accelerates the return of normal sensation in the treated area.

Mastectomy & Breast Surgery Recovery

Breast cancer surgery — including mastectomy, lumpectomy, and lymph node removal — can cause lymphedema (chronic arm swelling) when lymph nodes are removed or damaged. Early post-surgical lymphatic drainage is a key component of lymphedema prevention and management for breast cancer survivors.

Post-Surgery Recovery Timeline

Day 1–3

Rest & Monitoring

Focus on rest. Do not begin massage. Monitor for signs of infection or complications.

Day 3–7

First Session (with clearance)

First lymphatic drainage session with surgeon clearance. Gentle, focused on reducing acute swelling.

Week 1–2

Intensive Phase

Daily or every-other-day sessions. Rapid swelling reduction. Preventing fibrosis formation.

Week 3–6

Maintenance Phase

2–3 sessions per week. Continued swelling management. Scar tissue prevention.

Month 2–3

Ongoing Support

Weekly sessions. Final contouring support. Scar tissue management.

Frequently Asked Questions

How soon after BBL can I get lymphatic drainage?
Most surgeons recommend beginning lymphatic drainage 5–7 days after BBL, once the incision sites are closed and there are no signs of infection. Always get your surgeon's explicit clearance before beginning. Call us at (408) 668-6562 and we can coordinate with your surgeon's office if needed.
Can you come to my home after surgery?
Yes — this is one of our most requested services. We offer mobile post-surgery lymphatic drainage throughout the Bay Area (within 60km of Mountain View). We bring a professional massage table, fresh linens, and all necessary supplies. Mobile sessions start at $280 for 90 minutes, plus a travel fee based on your location.
How many sessions do I need after liposuction?
Most post-liposuction clients benefit from 6–12 sessions over the first 4–6 weeks. The exact number depends on the extent of your procedure, the areas treated, and how your body responds. We recommend starting with a series of 6 sessions and reassessing with your therapist.
Does post-surgery massage hurt?
No. Proper post-surgical lymphatic drainage uses very gentle, light-pressure techniques. The treated areas may be sensitive and tender, and our therapists are trained to work within your comfort level at all times. If you experience significant discomfort, please let your therapist know immediately.
